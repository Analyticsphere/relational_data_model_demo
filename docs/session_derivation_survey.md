# Connect Survey Session Derivation — feasibility & value

*Survey date: 2026-07-08 · Source: `schemas/{prod,stage}/CleanConnect/participants.json` + `data_dictionary/masterFile.csv`*

---

## Purpose & framing

The `response_sessions` enhancement (backlog §5) would add **one row per participant × survey administration**
(status / start / complete / wave), so that missingness can be classified (*not-administered* vs.
*not-answered* vs. *skipped-by-design*) and repeat administrations disambiguated.

**This is the one enhancement that partially deviates from the dictionary-driven structure of the model.**
Every other table keys on a dictionary concept; a `response_sessions` grain (participant × survey ×
administration) is a *derived* entity that the dictionary does not define as a first-class concept. So this
document is deliberately a **feasibility + value analysis, not a build spec** — we may decide *not* to build
a dedicated sessions table if the value doesn't justify the deviation. The questions it answers:

1. Is the session signal actually present in the data, and how completely? (feasibility)
2. How much does it deviate from the dictionary-driven structure, and can that be minimized?
3. What does it unlock that the base model can't do — and is that worth it? (value)

Quantitative throughout, in the style of the other survey docs. **No BigQuery queries were run** — analysis
is over the committed schemas and the dictionary only.

---

## Methodology

- **Source**: `data_dictionary/masterFile.csv` (13,123 rows) and `schemas/stage/CleanConnect/participants.json`
  (457 fields). Hierarchy columns forward-filled before use (spreadsheet fill-down).
- **Session-timing variables** identified by the dictionary's `Current Question Text` marker
  *"Autogenerated date/time stamp …"* (variable type `ISO`), classified `start` vs `complete` by the
  "Start"/"Complete"/"Submitted" keyword. (Note: complete-timestamps are labelled `…Sub` — *submitted* — not
  "complete", e.g. `D/T BOH Sub`; a naïve "complete" match misses them.)
- **Status variables** identified as questions whose response set includes a status-state concept
  (see §Status value set).

---

## What a session row would be

A `response_sessions` row is the **pivot of three per-survey autogenerated variables** the participant table
already carries — no new collection:

| Component | What it is | Example (Mouthwash) |
|---|---|---|
| **status** | 3-state completion flag | `949302066`-style concept → `0/1/2` |
| **started_at** | ISO timestamp, survey opened | `286191859` `D/T MW Surv Start` |
| **completed_at** | ISO timestamp, survey submitted | `195145666` `D/T MW Surv Sub` |

`is_complete = (status == submitted)`. One participant row → many session rows (one per survey administered).

## The status value set (uniform across surveys ✅)

Every survey's status concept draws from the **same global 3-value set** — verified in the dictionary's
format values:

| Response concept | Value / label |
|---|---|
| `972455046` | `0 = Not Started` |
| `615768760` | `1 = Started` |
| `231311385` | `2 = Submitted` |

This uniformity is the key enabler: one `status` interpretation works for all surveys, and `2 = Submitted`
is the reliable "the participant completed a full traversal" signal that makes missingness classifiable.

## Triad completeness per survey (feasibility ✅, with edges)

Of **20** `Primary Source = Survey` secondary sources, **15 have the full triad** (status + start + submit):

| Full triad? | Survey |
|---|---|
| ✅ | Background and Overall Health · Medications, Reproductive… · Smoking, Alcohol, and Sun · Where You Live and Work |
| ✅ | Mouthwash · Blood/Urine · Blood/Urine/Mouthwash · COVID-19 · Cancer Screening History |
| ✅ | Quality of Life · DHQ3 · Menstrual cycle · Social Security · 2024 Connect Experience · 2026 ROI Preference |
| ⚠️ partial | Diet Screener Questionnaire *(status only, no timestamps)* |
| ⚠️ none | COVID-19 (Post-Pandemic) · Self-Reported Cancer Dx · Menstrual Cycle *(casing dup of "Menstrual cycle")* · Messaging *(not a participant survey)* |

**Coverage: status 16/20 · start 15/20 · submit 15/20 · full triad 15/20.** The 5 gaps are mostly artifacts:
a **casing duplicate** (`Menstrual Cycle` vs `Menstrual cycle` — the lowercase one is full), **Messaging**
(not a survey), a **sub-questionnaire** (Self-Reported Cancer Dx), and two genuinely-incomplete instruments
(COVID-19 Post-Pandemic, Diet Screener). Effective coverage for real participant surveys is **~15/16**.

**The triad data physically lands in `participants`** (confirming derivability): of the session-timing and
status concepts, `participants.json` (stage) contains **62/77 timing** concepts and **15/16 status** concepts.
Deriving sessions is a **wide→long pivot of the participant row** — the same mechanic as the survey unpivot,
run over the participant table (which the survey unpivot currently excludes).

## The `wave` axis is the weak spot (honest caveat ⚠️)

The session grain nominally includes a **wave / administration index** (baseline / 3-month / 6-month). The
data does **not** support this cleanly for surveys:

- The **Baseline / Follow-up encoder** — `266600170 = 0 Baseline`, `496823485 = 1 Follow-up 1`,
  `650465111 = 2 Follow-up 2`, `303552867 = 3 Follow-up 3` — is used by only **4 questions**, all in the
  **event plane**: *Collection Details, Kit, Payment Round, Specimen Link*. **No survey uses it.**
- Survey "recurrence" is instead encoded as **distinct survey identities**: Quality of Life (≈3-month),
  DHQ3 (≈6-month), and COVID-19 vs COVID-19 (Post-Pandemic) are *separate secondary sources*, not waves of
  one survey.

**Implication:** a `wave` column on `response_sessions` would be **mostly constant (baseline)** for surveys,
with recurrence already captured by the survey identity. True per-round follow-up structure lives in the
**event plane** (backlog §9), keyed on `Round` — which is where a wave/administration index actually belongs.
So the "sessions unlock waves" argument is **weak for surveys** and really an argument for the event plane.

## Value analysis — what sessions unlock (and what the base model already does)

| Capability | Base model (responses only) | With `response_sessions` |
|---|---|---|
| "Was survey S submitted for participant P?" | **Already answerable** — the status concept is a dictionary question; unpivot it and it's a `responses` row | convenience: one denormalized column |
| Completion / response-rate metrics | derivable by grouping the status rows | direct, pre-joined |
| **True missingness** (submitted + reachable + no answer row) | **needs status + `skip_logic`** — status alone gives "submitted", `skip_logic` (§4) gives reachability | sessions packages the status half cleanly |
| Repeat-administration disambiguation (version Pattern 1) | ambiguous | resolves *if* a wave/attempt key exists — but see the wave caveat |
| Timing analytics (duration, time-of-day) | the ISO timestamps are dictionary concepts → unpivot them | pre-typed `started_at`/`completed_at` |

**Critical observation:** the status and timing signals are **themselves dictionary concepts**. If the
participant table's status/timing columns are unpivoted (like any survey table), completion and timing become
**ordinary `responses` rows** — no dedicated sessions table is *strictly required* to answer completion or
"submitted?" questions. `response_sessions` is a **denormalized convenience** over those rows, not a new
source of truth. This both (a) minimizes the deviation and (b) lowers the urgency.

---

## The deviation, and how to minimize it

| Concern | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Grain isn't a dictionary entity | True — session = participant × survey administration. But it is **derived entirely from dictionary concepts** (status + two ISO timestamps), so it doesn't introduce new authored structure. |
| Responses keying on `session_id` | **Optional.** `responses` need not carry `session_id`; they can join sessions on `(connect_id, secondary_source_concept_id)`. Keeping `session_id` off the fact preserves the pure concept-coordinate grain. |
| Extra ETL surface | One pivot step over `participants` (currently excluded from the unpivot). Bounded and mechanical. |

**Minimized form:** derive `response_sessions` as a **standalone dimension** (a wide→long pivot of the
participant triad), joined to `responses` on `(connect_id, survey)` — no `session_id` on the fact, no `wave`
column (until the event plane provides `Round`). This keeps the deviation to "a derived dimension over
existing dictionary concepts," which is consistent with the ground rule (an overlay, not a rewrite).

---

## Feasibility & value verdict

- **Feasibility: high.** The signal is present and uniform (status 3-value set), the triad is ~complete
  (15/16 real surveys), the data is in `participants`, and derivation is a bounded wide→long pivot. No new
  collection, no capability Connect lacks.
- **Deviation: minor and containable.** Sessions are derived from dictionary concepts; keep `session_id` off
  the fact and it's just an overlay dimension.
- **Value: real but *contingent* and partly redundant.** The headline win — **true missingness** — needs
  `skip_logic` (§4) to be worth much; without it, sessions only give "submitted vs not". And basic completion
  is *already* answerable by unpivoting the status concept into `responses`. The `wave` win is largely a
  mirage for surveys (it belongs to the event plane).

### Decision options

1. **Minimal now (recommended):** unpivot the participant **status + timing triad** into `responses` as
   ordinary rows (they're dictionary concepts). This delivers completion, timing, and the "submitted" half of
   missingness with **zero deviation** — no sessions table at all.
2. **Full sessions later, paired with §4:** build the `response_sessions` dimension only once `skip_logic`
   exists, so the missingness classification it enables is actually realizable. Derive it as an overlay
   dimension (no `session_id` on the fact, no `wave`).
3. **Wave/administration → event plane (§9), not here:** model per-round follow-up structure on `Round`,
   where the Baseline/Follow-up encoder actually lives — not as a survey `wave`.

**Recommendation:** do **Option 1 now** (it's within the dictionary-driven grain and cheap), and treat the
full `response_sessions` dimension as **deferred and coupled to §4** rather than a standalone build. The
sessions table earns its place only alongside skip logic.
